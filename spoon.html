Chinese | Transliteration: Yòng yī gēn kuàizi chīfàn de rén shì è de.
用一根筷子吃饭的人是饿的.
A person eating with one chopstick is hungry.
A single person or an incomplete effort is insufficient to achieve a task or satisfy a need. It emphasizes the importance of having the right tools or working together.
Korean | Transliteration: Namui bapgeureuseun nopa boigo jagi bapgeureuseun naja boinda.
남의 밥그릇은 높아 보이고 자기 밥그릇은 낮아 보인다.
Other people's rice bowls look high, and one's own rice bowl looks low.
People tend to think that others have more or are better off than themselves, even if it is not true. (Similar to "the grass is always greener on the other side"). Reflects on envy or dissatisfaction with one's own lot.
Spanish | Transliteration: La olla le dijo a la caldera: "Apártate, que me tiznas."
La olla le dijo a la caldera: "Apártate, que me tiznas."
The pot said to the kettle: "Get away, you are blackening me."
Used to describe a situation where someone criticizes another person for a fault that they themselves also possess. (Similar to "the pot calling the kettle black").
Italian | Transliteration: Ogni pazienza è vinta con la forchetta.
Ogni pazienza è vinta con la forchetta.
Every patience is overcome with the fork.
Even with great patience, there are limits, and eventually, action (like eating with a fork) must be taken. Can imply that even the most patient person will eventually reach their breaking point or need to act.
Hindi | Transliteration: Thali ka baingan.
थाली का बैंगन
An eggplant of the plate.
Refers to a person who is indecisive or inconsistent, easily swayed from one side to another, having no fixed stance or principles. (Like an eggplant that easily rolls around on a flat plate).
German | Transliteration: Viele Köche verderben den Brei.
Viele Köche verderben den Brei.
Many cooks spoil the broth.
When too many people are involved in a task, the result is often poor due to conflicting ideas or lack of clear direction.
