African (Zimbabwean) | Transliteration: Kusvika shumba yava nomunyori wayo, nyaya dzevhima dzicharumbidza murume.
Kusvika shumba yava nomunyori wayo, nyaya dzevhima dzicharumbidza murume
Until the lion has its own storyteller, the tale of the hunt will always glorify the hunter.
History or accounts of events are often told from the perspective of the powerful or the victors, and the voices of the less powerful or defeated are not heard.
Turkish | Transliteration: Er odunun iyisini eve getirir, aslan postun iyisini eve getirir.
Er odunun iyisini eve getirir, aslan postun iyisini eve getirir.
The man brings home the best wood, the lion brings home the best skin.
A truly capable or strong individual provides the best for their home or family through their efforts and strength.
Swahili | Transliteration: Simba mwenda pole ndiye mla nyama.
Simba mwenda pole ndiye mla nyama.
A slowly walking lion is the one that eats meat.
Patience and careful planning are more effective than haste and impulsiveness in achieving one's goals. (Emphasizes the deliberate nature of a successful hunter).
Somali | Transliteration: Libaax nin galo ma qaniino, nin aan gelin buu qaniinaa.
Libaax nin galo ma qaniino, nin aan gelin buu qaniinaa.
A lion does not bite the one who enters its den, it bites the one who does not enter.
Sometimes facing a difficult situation head-on is less dangerous than avoiding it or showing fear. Boldness can sometimes deter danger.
Malay | Transliteration: Terlepas dari mulut buaya, masuk ke mulut harimau.
Terlepas dari mulut buaya, masuk ke mulut harimau.
Escaped from the mouth of a crocodile, entered the mouth of a tiger.
To escape from one dangerous situation only to find oneself in an even worse one. (Similar to "out of the frying pan, into the fire").
Chinese | Transliteration: Yī shān bù róng èr hǔ.
一山不容二虎.
One mountain cannot accommodate two tigers.
Two equally powerful rivals cannot coexist in the same domain or position of power.
Chinese | Transliteration: Qí hǔ nán xià.
骑虎难下.
Hard to dismount a tiger you are riding.
To be in a difficult situation where it is hard to back down or stop, even if continuing is perilous.
