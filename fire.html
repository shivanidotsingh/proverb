Hindi | Transliteration: Aag lagne par kuan khodna
आग लगने पर कुआँ खोदना
Digging a well when fire breaks out.
Taking action only when disaster has already struck; acting too late.
Russian | Transliteration: Iz ognya da v polymya.
Из огня да в полымя
Out of the fire and into the flame.
To go from a bad or difficult situation to one that is even worse.
Russian | Transliteration: Mezhdu dvukh ogney.
Между двух огней.
Between two fires.
Caught between two dangers, difficulties, or opposing forces.
Arabic | Transliteration: Mithl al-nar ta'kul nafsaha in lam tajid ma ta'kuluhu
مثل النار تأكل نفسها إن لم تجد ما تأكله
Like fire that consumes itself if it finds nothing to consume.
People become destructive or consume themselves if they have nothing productive to occupy their energy; idleness can be destructive.
Japanese | Transliteration: Yakeishi ni mizu
焼け石に水
Water on a hot stone.
A small effort or action that has little or no effect on a large problem; a futile action.
Korean | Transliteration: Bullan jibe buchaejilhanda
불난 집에 부채질한다
Fanning a house on fire.
To make a bad situation worse; exacerbating a problem.
Italian
Chi gioca col fuoco si scotta.
Who plays with fire gets burned.
Engaging in risky behavior will inevitably lead to harm or negative consequences.
