Chinese | Transliteration: qiǎo fù nán wéi wú mǐ zhī chuī
巧妇难为无米之炊
Even a clever housewife cannot cook without rice.
One cannot achieve a task or goal, no matter how skilled they are, without the necessary basic resources or materials.
Japanese | Transliteration: Kome o kande aji o shiru.
米を噛んで味を知る.
Chew rice and know the taste.
Experience is the best teacher; you must try something yourself to truly understand it.
Korean | Transliteration: Ssal-i eopseumyeon bucheonimdo gumneunda.
쌀이 없으면 부처님도 굶는다.
If there is no rice, even Buddha starves.
Basic necessities are fundamental for survival; even the most powerful or spiritual beings cannot live without them.
Vietnamese | Transliteration: Có thực mới vực được đạo.
Có thực mới vực được đạo.
Only with food (rice) can one support religion.
Material needs must be met before one can pursue higher ideals, morality, or spiritual goals.
Vietnamese | Transliteration: Ăn cơm trước kẻng.
Ăn cơm trước kẻng.
To eat rice before the gong.
To act prematurely or to do something before the proper or expected time (often refers to marrying before the wedding ceremony).
Hindi | Transliteration: Chawal ke dane par naam likha hota hai.
दाने दाने पे लिखा है खाने वाले का नाम
The name of the eater is written on each grain.
Destiny or fate is predetermined; things happen according to a plan already set. (Refers to the belief that a person's destiny is inscribed on a grain of rice they are meant to eat).
Swahili | Transliteration: Mchele mmoja uliopo kwenye gunia moja hauwezi kufanya ugali.
Mchele mmoja uliopo kwenye gunia moja hauwezi kufanya ugali.
One grain of rice in one sack cannot make ugali (a stiff porridge from maize or rice flour).
One person alone cannot achieve a significant task; unity and collective effort are necessary.
Yoruba | Transliteration: Bí orí bá dára tí kò sí ìrẹsì, ebi á pa ni.
Bí orí bá dára tí kò sí ìrẹsì, ebi á pa ni.
If the head/destiny is good but there is no rice, one will starve.
Good fortune, potential, or intelligence is useless without basic necessities and resources.
Spanish (Latin America) | Transliteration: Quien no arriesga, no gana arroz.
Quien no arriesga, no gana arroz.
Who does not risk, does not earn rice.
Rewards, even basic sustenance, often require taking risks.
