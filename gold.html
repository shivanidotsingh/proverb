Polish | Transliteration: Nie wszystko złote, co się świeci.
Nie wszystko złote, co się świeci.
Not everything that shines is gold.
Appearance can be deceptive; something that looks valuable or good on the surface may not be so in reality. (A widely recognized proverb with equivalents in many languages, including Latin and Russian).
Turkish | Transliteration: Söz gümüşse sükût altındır.
Söz gümüşse sükût altındır.
If speech is silver, silence is gold.
While speaking can be valuable, remaining silent can often be more advantageous or wise. (Found in various languages, including Polish).
Russian | Transliteration: Zoloto ne vsyo, no vsyo nuzhdayetsya v zolote.
Золото не всё, но всё нуждается в золоте.
Gold is not everything, but everything needs gold.
While material wealth may not be the most important thing in itself, it is necessary for many aspects of life and achieving goals.
Chinese | Transliteration: Zhēn jīn bù pà huǒ liàn.
真金不怕火炼.
True gold is not afraid of the fire (melting pot).
Genuine quality, true character, or real ability will withstand testing and difficult circumstances.
Mongolian | Transliteration: Övchin garval amiinhaa tukhail bod, edgermeel altanhyoo tukhail bod.
Өвчин гарвал амийнхаа тухай бод, эдгэрмэл алтанхёо тухай бод.
If you are sick, think about your life; if you are better, think about your gold.
When facing serious issues like illness, focus on what truly matters (life/health); once the crisis is over, you can return to focusing on material concerns.
Latin | Transliteration: Auro hamo piscari.
Auro hamo piscari.
To fish with a golden hook.
To achieve something or gain an advantage by using wealth or bribery.
