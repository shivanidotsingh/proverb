Chinese | Transliteration: Yīgè rén bùnéng zǔzhǐ tàiyáng shēng qǐ.
一個人不能阻止太陽升起.
One person cannot stop the sun from rising.
Some events or forces are inevitable and beyond the control of any single individual. Highlights the power of natural cycles or destiny.
Swahili | Transliteration: Mvua haina mlango.
Mvua haina mlango.
Rain has no door. (Often contrasted with the sun's predictable path).
Some things, like rain (or misfortune/blessings), arrive unexpectedly and without warning, unlike the sun which follows a predictable course. It highlights the unpredictability of certain events in life.
Arabic | Transliteration: Lā yuḫfá nūr al-šams.
لا يخفى نور الشمس.
The light of the sun cannot be hidden.
Truth or something very obvious cannot be concealed or denied for long, regardless of attempts to hide it.
Turkish | Transliteration: Gök ağlamayınca yer gülmez.
Gök ağlamayınca yer gülmez.
If the sky doesn't cry, the earth won't laugh.
Sometimes, hardship or difficulty (the sky crying, i.e., rain) is necessary for growth, prosperity, or happiness (the earth laughing with abundant crops). It suggests that challenges can lead to positive outcomes.
African (General) | Transliteration: (See meaning)
Rain does not fall on one roof alone.
Challenges or misfortunes are not confined to a single person or place; they affect many.
Problems and difficulties are often shared or widespread, reminding individuals that they are not alone in their struggles. (A theme prevalent in many African cultures).
Estonian | Transliteration: Ka kõige mustem mure kaob valge liiva all.
Ka kõige mustem mure kaob valge liiva all.
Even the darkest sorrow disappears underneath the white sand.
Even the deepest sadness or trouble will eventually pass and be covered by time or healing, just as dark clouds eventually clear.
Arabic | Transliteration: waʿd as-saḥāb.
وعد السحاب.
A cloud's promise.
Refers to a promise that is unlikely to be fulfilled, or something that seems promising but ultimately delivers nothing, like a cloud that appears to hold rain but passes without a drop.
Akan (Ghana) | Transliteration: Osram nni agya, na omalley omum.
Osram nni agya, na ɔmmɛyɛ ɔmum.
The moon has no father, but it becomes pregnant (covered by clouds).
Some things happen due to circumstances or nature, not necessarily requiring a direct cause in the way we might expect. It speaks to phenomena that occur naturally or unexpectedly, like clouds covering the moon.
