Swedish
Berget födde en mus.
The mountain gave birth to a mouse.
Refers to a situation where a great effort, much anticipation, or a grand promise results in a very small or insignificant outcome.
Amharic | Transliteration: Tärara täraran ayəgätəməm; gən säw gən säwən yəgätəmal.
ተራራ ተራራን አይገጥምም፤ ሰው ግን ሰውን ይገጥማል።
Mountain does not meet mountain; but man meets man.
While physical obstacles may remain insurmountable, human relationships and encounters are inevitable and can overcome barriers.
Nepali | Transliteration: Himal hānse tārāgaṇ hānschhan, Himal roe tārāgaṇ runchhan.
हिमाल हाँसे तारागण हाँस्छन्, हिमाल रोए तारागण रुन्छन्।
If the Himalayas laugh, the stars laugh; if the Himalayas cry, the stars cry.
Events affecting something great or significant have a wide-ranging impact on associated or related entities.
Tibetan | Transliteration: Ri mtho yang lam yod.
རི་མཐོ་ཡང་ལམ་ཡོད་།
Although the mountain is high, there is a path.
Even the greatest difficulties or challenges can be overcome; there is always a way forward if one perseveres.
Chinese | Transliteration: Yú Gōng Yí Shān
愚公移山
The Foolish Old Man Moves the Mountains.
Refers to a story illustrating that with enough persistence and determination, even seemingly impossible tasks can be accomplished.
Pashto | Transliteration: Ghar ta che ookheezhe hamwaar shi.
غر ته چې اوخيژې هموار شي.
When you climb the mountain, it becomes flat.
Difficulties or challenges seem less daunting or become manageable once you actually face them or put effort into overcoming them.
German
Wer den Berg nicht besteigt, sieht nicht die Aussicht.
Who does not climb the mountain does not see the view.
Rewards, unique perspectives, or valuable insights are gained only by undertaking the necessary effort and facing the challenge.
Russian | Transliteration: Na gore Ararat rak svistnul.
На горе Арарат рак свистнул.
On Mount Ararat, a crayfish whistled.
Refers to something that is absolutely impossible or will never happen. (Uses a specific, prominent mountain).
Turkish | Transliteration: Dağdan kopan çığ gibi.
Dağdan kopan çığ gibi.
Like an avalanche broken off from the mountain.
Describes something happening suddenly, rapidly, and with overwhelming force.
