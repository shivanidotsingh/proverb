Proverbs about Ice 🧊

Meaning Group: Calmness, Patience, Self-Control
Ha is i magen
Language: Swedish | Literal Translation: To have ice in the stomach | Meaning: To be calm, patient, cool-headed.
Meaning Group: Starting Interaction / Overcoming Awkwardness
Break the ice
Language: English | Meaning: To start a conversation or interaction in an awkward situation.
Bryta isen
Language: Swedish | Literal Translation: To break the ice | Meaning: Same as the English idiom.
Meaning Group: Being in a Risky or Precarious Situation
On thin ice
Language: English | Meaning: In a risky situation; likely to get into trouble.
På tunn is
Language: Swedish | Literal Translation: On thin ice | Meaning: Same as the English idiom.
Meaning Group: Emotional Coldness / Unfriendliness
Cold as ice
Language: English | Meaning: Emotionally unfeeling, distant, or unfriendly.
Icy stare / Icy glare
Language: English | Meaning: A look that is very unfriendly or disapproving.
Kall som is
Language: Swedish | Literal Translation: Cold as ice | Meaning: Lacking warmth (literal or emotional).
Meaning Group: Postponing or Suspending Something
Put something on ice
Language: English | Meaning: To postpone or suspend a plan or project temporarily.
Meaning Group: Disappearing Quickly / Tension or Anger Melting Away
बर्फ की तरह पिघलना
Language: Hindi | Transliteration: Barf ki tarah pighalna | Literal Translation: To melt like ice | Meaning: To disappear very quickly, or for anger/tension to dissolve.
બરફની જેમ પીગળવું
Language: Gujarati | Transliteration: Barafni jem pigalvu | Literal Translation: To melt like ice | Meaning: Same meaning as the Hindi phrase.
ਬਰਫ਼ ਵਾਂਗ ਪਿਘਲ ਜਾਣਾ
Language: Punjabi | Transliteration: Baraf vangh pighal jaana | Literal Translation: To melt like ice | Meaning: Same meaning as the Hindi phrase.
