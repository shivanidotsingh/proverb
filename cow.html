Indian (Hindi) | Transliteration: Jiski laathi uski bhains.
जिसकी लाठी उसकी भैंस।
Whose stick, his buffalo.
Might is right; whoever has the power or force gets to keep or control things.
Swahili | Tachoni (Kenya): Okhama ing'ombe nolengela iketi.
Ukikamua ng'ombe ya wenyewe huwe ukiangalia langoni.
You milk someone else's cow while watching the gate.
When doing something wrong or illicitly, you are always watchful and fearful of being caught.
Jamaican Patois
If goat did know di size a him battyhole him wouldn't swallow mango seed.
If a goat knew the size of its anus it would not swallow a mango seed.
Know your limits; do not take on more than you can handle, or you will suffer the consequences. (A vivid and memorable proverb about understanding one's capabilities).
Arabic | Transliteration: Tawakaltu ala Allah wa 'aqaltu jamalī.
توكلت على الله وعقلت جملي
I have put my trust in God, and I have tied my camel.
Combine faith or hope with practical effort and preparation. Trust in a higher power, but also take necessary precautions and actions yourself.
Arabic | Transliteration: La yaḍḥaku al-jamal min ḥadabat akhihi.
لا يضحك الجمل من حدبة أخيه
A camel does not laugh at the hump of its brother.
Do not mock or criticize others for their flaws or misfortunes, as you may have similar ones yourself, or face similar challenges.
Amharic (Ethiopia) | Transliteration: Yägētā bägäräb yāgär bämān yabäzā.
የጌታ በገረብ የአገር በማን ያበዛል።
The master's [cow] increases in the cowshed, the country's increases by whom?
It is easy for an individual to increase their own wealth, but increasing the prosperity of a whole nation requires collective effort and leadership. (Uses the image of increasing cows as a metaphor for prosperity).
