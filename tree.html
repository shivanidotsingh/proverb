Akan (Ghana/Ewe) | Transliteration: Nunya, adidoe, asi metunee o.
Nunya, adidoe, asi metunee o.
Wisdom is like a baobab tree; no one individual can embrace it.
True wisdom is vast and multifaceted, beyond the complete grasp of any single person. It emphasizes the value of collective knowledge and humility in the pursuit of understanding. (The Baobab tree is native to parts of Africa, including the region of the Akan and Ewe people).
Vietnamese | Transliteration: Tre già măng mọc.

This proverb speaks to the continuity and renewal of generations. Even as the old pass on, new life emerges to continue the lineage or tradition.
