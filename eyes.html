To deceive or fool someone.

What one sees or observes directly influences what one says, sometimes leading to unintended words, errors, or gossip.
Malayalam | Transliteration: Kannullappol kanninte vilayariyilla.

Russian | Transliteration: Glaza boyatsya, a ruki delayut.

The eyes are afraid, but the hands do it.
Don't be intimidated or afraid by the apparent difficulty of a task; just begin the work, and your hands (actions) will accomplish it.
Arabic | Transliteration: Al-ʿayn lā taʿlū ʿala l-ḥājib.

The eye does not rise above the eyebrow.
One should know and respect their position or rank; a subordinate should not try to surpass or act superior to their superior.

Envy is harmful or painful to the person who harbors it.

One's eyes and facial expressions can convey feelings and thoughts more powerfully than spoken words.

Believe only what you see with your own eyes; firsthand experience is the most reliable source of truth.
German

Problems or tasks are often better addressed with the input and perspective of more than one person.

Affection or concern for someone or something tends to decrease with physical distance or lack of contact.
