Portuguese (Brazil) | Transliteration: Está chovendo canivetes.
Está chovendo canivetes.
It's raining pocket knives.
A humorous and vivid way to say it's raining very heavily. It's an exaggerated image to emphasize the intensity of the downpour.
Afrikaans (South Africa/Namibia) | Transliteration: Ou vrouens met knopkieries reen.
Ou vrouens met knopkieries reen.
It's raining old women with knobkerries (traditional sticks).
Another colorful and unusual expression for very heavy rain, creating a humorous mental image of old women falling from the sky with their walking sticks.
Turkish | Transliteration: Karpuz kabuğu denize düşmeden suya girilmez.
Karpuz kabuğu denize düşmeden suya girilmez.
You don't enter the water before the watermelon rind falls into the sea.
A quirky way of saying you shouldn't swim until the water is warm enough, marked by the time watermelons are ripe and their rinds might be discarded into the sea. It signifies waiting for the right, naturally indicated time for something.
Japanese | Transliteration: Niwaka-ame de ishi o nagaru.
俄雨で石を流る.
Washing stones with a sudden shower.
Attempting to achieve a significant result with insufficient or fleeting effort. A sudden shower (niwaka-ame) is not enough to wash away stones, just as a small, inconsistent effort won't accomplish a large task.
