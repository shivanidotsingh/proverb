German
Eulen nach Athen tragen.
To carry owls to Athens.
Doing something completely unnecessary, redundant, or superfluous (Athens was associated with wisdom and its coins featured owls).

Старого воробья на мякине не проведёшь.
You can't fool an old sparrow with chaff.
An experienced or cunning person is not easily deceived or tricked.

To have one's fried tofu snatched by a kite.

笨鸟先飞
The clumsy bird flies first.
Those who are less talented or gifted need to work harder or start earlier than others to succeed or keep up.
Hindi | Transliteration: Chidiya khet chug gayi phir pachhtaye kya hot?
चिड़िया खेत चुग गई फिर पछताए क्या होत?
If the bird has already eaten the field, then what is the use of regretting?
There is no use regretting or being concerned after the damage has already been done or the opportunity is lost.
Spanish | Transliteration: Cría cuervos y te sacarán los ojos.
Cría cuervos y te sacarán los ojos.
Raise crows and they will peck out your eyes.
If you help or nurture ungrateful or malicious people, they will eventually turn against and harm you.
German | Transliteration: Ein Spatz in der Hand ist besser als eine Taube auf dem Dach.
Ein Spatz in der Hand ist besser als eine Taube auf dem Dach.
A sparrow in the hand is better than a pigeon on the roof.
It is better to have something small and certain than to risk it for the chance of something bigger that you may not get. (Features both a sparrow and a pigeon/dove).

Hungarian | Transliteration: Bagoly mondja verébnek, hogy nagyfejű.

The owl tells the sparrow that it is big-headed.
Similar to "the pot calling the kettle black," this proverb is used when someone criticizes another for a fault they themselves possess.
